User Journey & Conversion Funnel Analysis
Overview
This project analyzes customer behavior across an e-commerce purchase funnel using Google BigQuery SQL and Power BI. The objective was to identify where users drop off during the purchasing journey, evaluate conversion performance, and generate actionable business insights to improve customer acquisition and revenue generation.
The analysis was conducted using the Google Analytics sample e-commerce dataset and follows a complete business intelligence workflow from data extraction and transformation to dashboard development and insight generation.
Business Objective
The primary goals of this project were to:
Analyze customer progression through the e-commerce funnel.
Identify major conversion bottlenecks and drop-off points.
Evaluate revenue performance across channels, products, and customer segments.
Compare behavior between new and returning customers.
Assess device-level performance and conversion efficiency.
Develop interactive dashboards for business decision-making.

Key Findings
Less than 2% of users completed a purchase.
The largest drop-off occurred between product browsing and checkout.
Checkout abandonment remained a major conversion challenge.
Returning customers generated significantly more revenue than first-time visitors.
Desktop users accounted for the majority of revenue and conversions.
Direct and Organic Search channels were the strongest acquisition sources.
A small number of product categories generated a disproportionately large share of total revenue.
